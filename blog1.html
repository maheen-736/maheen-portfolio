IELTS Journey: Student Experiences to Study Abroad

Each student's journey to achieving their dream of studying abroad is unique. Hear from real students as they share their IELTS preparation stories and how they overcame the challenges of international education.

Students' IELTS Journey: Diverse Experiences, Shared Aspirations

IELTS (International English Language Testing System) is a mandatory exam for students wishing to study abroad. It assesses your English language proficiency across four skills: Listening, Reading, Writing, and Speaking. While the journey to achieving a high IELTS score can be challenging, the rewards of studying at an international university make it all worthwhile.

Ayesha's Story: Striving for Perfection in IELTS

Ayesha, a student from Rawalpindi, decided to pursue her Master's degree in the United Kingdom. With dreams of studying at a top-ranked university, she knew she needed an exceptional IELTS score to make her application stand out.

Preparation Journey

Ayesha enrolled in an IELTS coaching center and dedicated herself to daily practice. She was particularly challenged by the speaking section, as she found it difficult to speak fluently under time pressure. To overcome this, she joined English-speaking groups and participated in mock interviews with a mentor.

"My advice for IELTS candidates is to not just focus on the academic side, but also immerse yourself in English every dayâ€”whether through movies, podcasts, or casual conversations with native speakers," she says.

Result

Ayesha achieved a band score of 8.5, which helped her secure a spot in her dream university. She emphasized the importance of consistent practice and confidence building in her journey to success.

Omar's Struggles: From 6.5 to 7.5 in IELTS

Omar, a student from Islamabad, wanted to pursue his studies in Canada. Initially, he scored a 6.5 band in his first IELTS attempt, which was just shy of the required 7.0 for his chosen university. Despite feeling disappointed, Omar didn't give up and worked harder to achieve a better score.

Preparation Tips

After his first attempt, Omar realized that his listening and writing sections were the weakest. He began practicing listening to English podcasts and watching documentaries in English. For writing, he focused on understanding essay structures and practiced writing essays on a variety of topics.

"IELTS isn't just about knowing English. You also need to understand the format and learn how to manage your time effectively during the exam," says Omar.

Result

On his second attempt, Omar scored a 7.5, which made him eligible for his desired Canadian universities. He attributes his success to practicing regularly and analyzing his weak areas critically.

Sarah and Ali: A Group Study Success Story

Sarah and Ali, two friends from Islamabad, decided to prepare for IELTS together. They found that group study sessions worked best for them, as they could help each other out and tackle the exam's challenges more effectively. Their primary focus was on improving the writing and speaking sections.

Group Study Technique

They set aside time every weekend for joint practice, where they would take turns writing essays, critique each other's work, and practice speaking topics with a timer. They also made a pact to speak only in English during their study sessions to improve their fluency.

"Having a study partner keeps you motivated and helps you cover more ground. If you get stuck, your partner might help you see things differently," says Sarah.

Result

Sarah and Ali both achieved band scores of 7.0, which were enough for them to secure spots at universities in Australia. They believe that their teamwork and mutual support were key to their success.

Jamil's Solo Preparation: Self-Study and Discipline

Jamil, a student from Islamabad chose to prepare for IELTS through self-study. With no access to a coaching center, he relied on online resources and books to guide his preparation. Despite the challenges, Jamil remained disciplined and followed a structured routine.

Self-Study Resources

Jamil used various IELTS preparation apps, YouTube channels, and online courses to hone his skills. He particularly enjoyed the flexibility of studying at his own pace, though he had to be strict with himself to avoid distractions.

"Self-study requires a lot of discipline. But with the right resources and a set schedule, you can do it on your own," Jamil says.

Result

Jamil scored an impressive 8.0 band in IELTS, securing admission to his desired university in the United States. He believes that the key to his success was consistent practice and making use of high-quality online resources.
